Midnight Passport

A named, self-sovereign, privacy-preserving identity on the Midnight network — without seeds, raw addresses, or required operators on the critical path. This page sets out the user-facing promises we are making, the components we have to build to keep them, and the design space underneath each one.

Section 1

The promises

Ten user-facing invariants that v1.0 must hold. Each is stated as something a user (or ecosystem partner) can rely on, with falsifiable invariants underneath. Click any card to see its full statement, the testable invariants, and the components that serve it.

How we got here

From the secure-onboarding six to the working ten

Pass 1 of the planning work started from the six principles in the secure-onboarding document — Key-bound TEE; ZK witnesses, Single seed, three wallets, Chain abstraction via intent, One key per device, Privacy-by-design identity, and Seedless UX. The ten promises above are not a different vision — they are the same six pressure-tested for falsifiability, with mechanism statements peeled off and previously-implicit assumptions made explicit so each can be individually tested. Every promise traces back to something the original document said or assumed; nothing here is invented.

Original principle Disposition New mapping
#1 Key-bound TEE; ZK witnesses Split, abstracted P6 TEE specificity removed; ZK witnesses move to mechanism layer.
#2 Single seed, three wallets Dropped Implementation specification, not a v1.0 invariant.
#3 Chain abstraction via intent Preserved P10
#4 One key per device Rewritten, split P3P6P7 Mechanism statement; surfaces multi-device peerage, no exfiltration, and scoped grants as separate principles.
#5 Privacy-by-design identity Kept, renamed P9 Renamed for sharper testability.
#6 Seedless UX Split P1P5 No-mnemonic UX is P1; total-loss recovery without seed is P5.

Five made explicit — promises that were implicit in the original document and surfaced as their own principles by Pass 1's pressure-testing.

  • P2Named— the original mentioned alice.midnight throughout as the user-facing handle, but never elevated naming to a principle.
  • P3Peer-device— implicit in #4; multi-device peerage was the premise of the device-add flow.
  • P4Revoke-and-continue— implicit in the multi-device story; the QR-code device-add flow shadowed a revoke-old-and-continue mode.
  • P7Scoped grants— function-call keys appeared as a sub-feature of #4 but were never named as their own primitive.
  • P8Chain-only— assumed throughout the document (no operator was required for any flow), but never stated as a constraint.

Section 5

The delivery

Two parallel deliveries, not two teams. The October demo is what runs on a screen by year-end. The standards-and-spec body is what survives external review and becomes feature-complete v1.0. They are symbiotic — same primitives evolve in lockstep, demo findings push back into specs, and spec drafts inform demo choices.

How they connect

Four primitives, two complementary roles

Each shared primitive plays a complementary role on each side — the demo's implementation IS what the standards body ratifies. One body of code; both sides feed feature-complete v1.0.

C1

Account-custody contract

Demo

Deploys one Compact contract per account. Mirrors the custody-feasibility experiment; on-chain by Phase A.

Standards

Ratified as MIP-3 (multi-key account contract). Co-design with Lace; formal-methods sign-off post-ratification.

C5

Signing primitive

Demo

Threshold Schnorr-on-Jubjub via a partner-operated MPC committee. Verified end-to-end against experiments/redjubjub-wallet/.

Standards

Ratified as MIP-1 (key derivation paths). Per-device on-device signing the standards-path target.

C8

Domain-separation registry

Demo

Central registry document; cryptographer review pre-October so every hash use site is prefixed before the demo ships.

Standards

STD-03 prerequisite. Unblocks every MIP that hashes user state — credentials, signing, naming.

C16

Wallet local storage

Demo

Holds session and auth state to the MPC provider. Encryption envelope keyed off WebAuthn-derived material on the device.

Standards

Encryption envelope and storage substrate spec'd against multi-platform wrapping-key APIs (iOS Keychain, Android StrongBox, browser IndexedDB).
