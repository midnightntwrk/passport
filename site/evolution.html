ARC · Midnight Passport · Plan update
Updated 2026/04/21

The plan evolved.

A cleaner path to the same MVP. Same product, same June 2026 target, same user experience — a different custody model underneath.

What we set out to build

A named, self-sovereign, privacy-preserving identity on Midnight — alice.midnight, passkey onboarding, three wallets derived from one seedless root, no raw addresses, no gas-token purchase screens. Six design principles drawn from the secure-onboarding-design spec, a standards-track pipeline of MIPs the wider ecosystem can adopt, and an MVP delivered by the end of June 2026 under a weekly-demo cadence. That vision is unchanged.

What the research surfaced

Two things emerged from the DUST funding and transaction-sponsorship research earlier in April. First, a gap in the original fee-payment story that needed closing before the MVP could ship. Second, a cleaner architectural path that avoids standing up an MPC federation at all. Once the new sponsorship primitives were on the table, the cost of operating a threshold-signing committee — infrastructure, re-sharing cadence, Byzantine monitoring, committee-recovery UX — became hard to justify when a simpler route reached the same user-facing outcome.

What we chose — the current MVP path

The user's passkey is the signing root. All cryptographic operations run client-side in the browser: key derivation, signing of Midnight intents with the NightExternal key, proof construction. The Midnight node verifies the intent signature at submission time; Compact contracts — including the name registry — bind ownership to the signing pubkey and trust the node's intent-signature check, so MVP 1 performs no in-circuit signature verification. The account provider is a lighter service — JWT issuance, name resolution, rate limiting, optional sponsorship relaying — with no custody authority and no key material. Midnight Passport is decentralised from day one. The trade-off is scope: a limited browser and operating-system matrix at MVP, and account recovery deferred behind genuine multi-device support. One device, one account at MVP 1. The 1-of-N in-circuit Schnorr primitive preserved in the experiments returns under MVP-09 as the multi-device foundation.

What we kept — the original plan

Plan B — the FROST threshold-signing committee with universal browser support and federation-mediated recovery by design — is preserved in full. It becomes the foundation for Milestone 2 cross-chain support, where the threshold-signing substrate is the natural fit. It also remains the fallback: if Plan A hits a blocking risk, the Plan B artefacts are still current and still reviewable.

What survives unchanged

  • The end-of-June-2026 MVP target
  • The alice.midnight name registry (MVP-04)
  • Passkey onboarding and the seedless UX contract
  • No seed phrase, no raw address, no gas-token purchase screen
  • Single seed, three wallets (Shielded, Night, Dust) via CIP-1852
  • The six secure-onboarding design principles
  • The weekly-demo cadence; every Friday, one thing that works
  • The MIP standards track — key derivation, address format, domain separation
  • The in-circuit signature work from experiments/redjubjub-wallet* (returns as the 1-of-N multi-device foundation under MVP-09)
