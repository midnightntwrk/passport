## 2. Logo System

### 2.1 Logo Variants (in order of visual weight)

| Variant | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Full word marque** (butterfly + "Input \| Output Group") | Strongest corporate expression ‚Äî hero sections, formal documents. |
| **Word marque** ("Input \| Output Group", no butterfly) | Typographic / formal contexts without the butterfly. |
| **Primary logo** ("Input \| Output") | Referring to the company itself. |
| **IO Group marque** ("I\|O Group") | Compact / space-constrained layouts. Min width **130 px**. |
| **IO marque** ("I\|O") | Iconic reinforcement ‚Äî must always appear with surrounding context linking to Input Output. |
| **Butterfly marque** | Iconic reinforcement ‚Äî same rule as IO marque. |

### 2.2 Clearance

- Clear space = **height of the "O"** from the word marque on all sides.
- Full word marque minimum width: **300 px**.
- IO Group marque minimum width: **130 px**.

### 2.3 Colour Modes

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| **Positive** | Full colour on light background (butterfly red + black text + "Group" in red). |
| **Negative** | Full colour on dark background (butterfly white/red + white text + "Group" in red). |
| **Black** | Mono black on light background. |
| **White** | Mono white on dark background. |

### 2.4 Logo Don'ts

- Do **not** rearrange elements.
- Do **not** scale the butterfly independently.
- Do **not** stretch or compress.
- Do **not** contain in a shape.
- Do **not** apply effects (shadows, glows, outlines).
- Do **not** recolour to unapproved colour ways.
- Do **not** place on busy / low-contrast backgrounds.

### 2.5 Partnership Lock-ups

- Use the **IO Group marque** (not the full logo with butterfly).
- Separate with an **√ó** character.
- Both word marks at the **same height**.
- Follow standard clearance rules.
- No added colours. No busy backgrounds.

### 2.6 "Built with / on / by" Attribution

Three lock-up phrases exist: `BUILT WITH I|O Group`, `BUILT ON I|O Group`, `BUILT BY I|O Group`. Use these below product logos for attribution (e.g. "Cardano ‚Äî BUILT WITH I|O Group").

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Typeface Stack

| Priority | Typeface | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | **IO Group Sans** (bespoke) | Headlines, hero text, all branded display type. |
| **Secondary** | **Barlow** | Body copy, long-form text, general communication. |
| **System fallback** | **Bricolage Grotesque** | Live-text contexts (emails, editable docs) where brand fonts can't embed. |

> **For web / UI implementations:** If IO Group Sans is unavailable, substitute with Barlow for headings and Bricolage Grotesque for body. Both are available on Google Fonts.

### 4.2 Hierarchy Rules

| Element | Typeface | Weight | Case | Letter-spacing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Large Headline** | IO Group Sans | Medium | Title Case | -20 |
| **Small Headline** | IO Group Sans | Medium | Title Case | -20 |
| **Sub Heading** | Barlow | Light | ALL CAPS | 100 |
| **Page Furniture** | Barlow | Light | ALL CAPS | 100 |
| **Body Copy** | Barlow | Regular | Sentence case | 0 |

### 4.3 Bespoke Display Numbers

IO Group Display Numbers are **graphic elements** (bold geometry with distinctive cut-outs). Use only for: section markers, contents numbering, prominent statistics. **Not** for inline text.

## 5. Iconography

### 5.1 Graphic Icons

- Expressive, detailed line-art icons (spirals, starburst, eye, venn, grid, etc.).
- Use for: storytelling, presentations, visual emphasis.
- **Do not** use for actions, labels, or navigation.

### 5.2 Functional Icons

- Clarity-first, minimal icons for UI.
- Use for: navigation, status indicators, interactive controls.
- Must be used consistently across digital products.
- **Do not** use in the expressive manner of graphic icons.

## 6. Art Direction

### 6.1 Photography Principles

**Human photography:**
- Cinematic, close-up or chest-up.
- Warm orange-red directional light fading into deep blue / teal / black shadows.
- Realistic skin texture (pores, freckles) ‚Äî not over-airbrushed.
- Casual / tech-wear clothing ‚Äî no suits.
- Subtle schematic overlays (blueprint lines, data grids, wireframe arcs) glowing in red-orange and cyan.
- Background: minimal, gradient-driven, fading to darkness.

**Nature photography:**
- Organic forms (coral, flowers, jellyfish, cellular structures) viewed through a scientific lens.
- Vivid orange-red glow contrasting deep indigo shadows.
- Subtle technological overlays (network lines, geometric filaments, pulsing nodes).

### 6.2 Schematic Overlays

Thin, technical line-work layered onto gradients, photography, or video:
- Thin glowing lines, data grids, coordinate markers, circular arcs, hexagonal meshes, wireframe nodes.
- Must feel technical but not literal ‚Äî add depth without overpowering content.
- Use sparingly.

### 6.3 Illustrations

- Core brand palette only.
- Confident, minimal: bold shapes, negative space, controlled colour.
- Translate complex ideas into clear visuals.
- Dark backgrounds preferred (black or near-black).

### Dark-First Design

IOG's visual identity is **dark-first**. Default backgrounds should be `#000000` or near-black with the Enlightenment gradient. Light backgrounds (`#FFFFFF` or light grey) are used for secondary content panels, documentation, and internal tools.

### Accessibility Checklist

- White text on Infared (`#E52321`): contrast ratio ‚âà 4.6:1 ‚Äî passes AA for large text only. For body text, use White on Black.
- Infared on Black: contrast ratio ‚âà 4.0:1 ‚Äî use for headlines / large text only.
- For body text, always pair White/Black for maximum contrast.
- Acid Green on Black: good contrast ‚Äî suitable for accent labels.
- Ultraviolet on Black: verify contrast; may need lighter variant for small text.

## 10. Agent Behavioural Rules

1. **Always default to dark backgrounds** (`#000000`) with the Enlightenment gradient unless the context specifically calls for light mode.
2. **Primary colours first.** Only introduce secondary colours for functional differentiation (charts, division identity). Tertiary colours for data visualisation accents only.
3. **Headlines in IO Group Sans** (or Barlow fallback), **body in Barlow**. Never use decorative or serif fonts.
4. **Respect logo clearance.** When placing the logo programmatically, pad by the height of the "O" character on all sides.
5. **Use "Input Output Group" on first mention**, then "IO" or product names thereafter.
6. **Gradients are atmospheric**, not decorative fills. They fade from black into colour ‚Äî never solid-to-solid colour transitions.
7. **Schematic overlays are optional enhancements** ‚Äî thin lines, low opacity, technical feel. Do not overuse.
8. **Division logos only appear on their designated gradient or black.** Never on white or off-brand backgrounds.
9. **No effects on logos** ‚Äî no drop shadows, glows, outlines, or shape containers.
10. **Photography style:** cinematic, warm/cool contrast, schematic overlays, dark atmospheric backgrounds. Never stock-photo aesthetic.
