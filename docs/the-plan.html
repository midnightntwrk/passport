Standards track
06 / 08
MIPs pipeline · parallel to build

Eight standards.
Co-authored, not shelved.

Midnight has no formal improvement-proposal process yet; the Passport MIPs are both the vehicle for interoperability and the forcing function that creates that process. Every MIP ships with a named external co-author — unilateral drafts become shelfware.

STD-01 and STD-02 ship during the MVP window. ECO-01 (connection) and ECO-02 (credentials) are the highest-priority standards — they define how the ecosystem plugs together. MIP-3 (naming) ships in Step 1.1 post-MVP.

Delivery contract
08 / 08
Weekly cadence · June MVP · 90-day beats

The weekly commit
is the contract.

If a week's intended deliverable slips, the rule is: show the next thing that works rather than a broken thing. The June MVP is the aspiration; demonstrable progress every week is the contract.

Weekly

One demoable thing

Every Friday Karmel runs a demo with whichever artefact moved. The console is the visible through-line; behind it the engine lane progresses weekly. "What doesn't work yet" is on the slide — always.

End of June

End-to-end MVP demo

Passkey login → account dashboard → wallet connects (Lace or stub) → capability grant approved → transaction signed → submitted to devnet. Plus a recovery sad-path. Plus "what's next" pointing to Step 1.1.

90-day cadence

Step 1.1 → 2 → 3

After June, each 90-day cycle adds federation, name registry, multi-device on-chain, on-device cryptography, or credentials — in the order that clears the next principle in the migration narrative.

The plan is accountable at three time scales: weekly, 12-week MVP, and 90-day beats thereafter.
